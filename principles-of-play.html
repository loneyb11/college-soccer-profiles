Attacking Priciples

Creating Space

  • Defenders look to drop and provide depth when supporting
  • Wide players look to get as wide as the field provides.
  • Midfielders look to go as high as they in relation to the space. Not typically as high as forwards.
  • Forwards strecth the field and by being as high as the last defender/offside-line. In some circumstances they may stray to an offside possion.


Support

  • Each player is requied to provide support to the ball carrier within their immediate area and when the space allows for a longer option.
  • Support can come form many angles. Forwards can provide support, wide players provide support, and players behind the ball provide support.
  • Support can be interpreted as proving a passing option, providing a passing an option to maintain possession and proving cover by being prepared to defend in the transitonal moment.

SA United coaches need to have the mindset that the entire club is a ‘team’ and it not about mine or I. Coaches understand the bigger picture, focusing on quality training sessions, development and learning over winning games. Each coach understands that SA United is a family, and we all share the same goals and aspirations.


Coaches have an open growth mindset and dedicate themselves to continually learning, evolving and improving their craft.


1

Pre-Game

Do not over coach. Coaches should be prepared prior to the game and have with 2-3 objectives for the to focus on.

2

During The Game

Coach the player OFF the ball. Do not joystick the players and allow the player on the ball the freedom and creativity to decide what they think the best action is given the scenario.


Organize the units (defense, midfield, attack) to ensure the balance and shape are good and ready to deal with counter attacks.
Do not make comments on every action.

Be aware of your tone – do not scream and shout at players.
Do not use rhetorical questions to make a point. Use moments in the game to teach the players, save comments or constructive criticism to post game film review.

Coaches are encouraged to ask open ended questions that are designed to encourage the player to think about the problem and provide a solution.

3

Feedback

The purpose of providing feedback is to improve the situation and/or persons performance. Being overally critical or harsh will likely do the opposite.


Sometimes it might be good motivation for the player if you ask for feedback.


Provide meaningful feedback after substituting a player. Provide constructive criticism and do not forget to provide praise for good play the player has produced.


4

Half Time

Young players will lose focus if coaches talk to much. Provide 2-3 coaching points and areas that the team needs to improve in the 2nd half.

5

Post-Game

Post game keep your talk short. Again, do not overly criticize. Shake hands or high five each player and let them know we will discuss certain themes from the game at training next week.

“No coach ever stops learning. That’s what makes the great coaches. They strive to learn more every day, and they never stop asking questions.”

- Jurgen Klopp, Liverpool FC Manager
