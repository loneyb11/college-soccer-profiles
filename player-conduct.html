PLAYER CONDUCT

Game day demeanor is an important element of the coaching environment and culture. Our coaches are representing our club and our seen as role models. Therefore, they must set and adhere to certain standards and always maintain professionalism.


Just like a training session we see the game as a continuation of the learning process. The game is an opportunity for our players to put into practice what they have learnt during the week. Our coaches therefore must facilitate the game and allow the players to make their own decisions. We will not leave our players on their own and will always aim to provide meaningful instruction and coaching points, however, we will give our players responsibilities to make their own decisions. SA United coaches need to have the mindset that the entire club is a ‘team’ and it not about mine or I. Coaches understand the bigger picture, focusing on quality training sessions, development and learning over winning games. Each coach understands that SA United is a family, and we all share the same goals and aspirations.


Coaches have an open growth mindset and dedicate themselves to continually learning, evolving and improving their craft.


“No coach ever stops learning. That’s what makes the great coaches. They strive to learn more every day, and they never stop asking questions.”

- Jurgen Klopp, Liverpool FC Manager
